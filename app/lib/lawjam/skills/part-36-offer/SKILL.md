# /part-36-offer

1. Identify the forum. CPR Part 36 applies in the civil courts (County Court, High Court, Court of Appeal). It does **not** apply in the Employment Tribunal.
2. In the ET, the settlement vehicles are (a) ACAS COT3 — binding settlement brokered through ACAS, and (b) Calderbank / "without prejudice save as to costs" offers — which carry weaker costs consequences than Part 36 because the ET costs regime is restrictive (ETR 2013 rule 76).
3. Apply the correct mechanics for the chosen vehicle. Part 36 has strict formal requirements (CPR 36.5); a defective Part 36 offer falls back to Calderbank effect at best.
4. Draft. Surface the costs consequences in the covering note so the recipient understands them.

# Settlement offer drafting

## Purpose

A well-formed settlement offer shifts the costs risk. The choice of vehicle determines what costs consequences flow if the offer is not beaten at trial. The wrong vehicle wastes the costs pressure entirely.

## Decision tree

```
Is the claim in the Employment Tribunal?
├── Yes → ACAS COT3 (if ACAS is involved) OR Calderbank offer
│         (Part 36 does not apply)
└── No (County Court / High Court) → CPR Part 36 (default)
                                     OR Calderbank if a non-Part-36 effect is wanted
```

## Part 36 — formal requirements (CPR 36.5)

A Part 36 offer must:

1. Be in writing.
2. Make clear it is made pursuant to Part 36.
3. Specify a period of not less than 21 days within which the defendant will be liable for the claimant's costs if the offer is accepted ("the relevant period").
4. State whether it relates to the whole of the claim or to part of it, and to issues that arise.
5. State whether it takes into account any counterclaim.

If any of these are missing, the offer is not a Part 36 offer and the enhanced costs consequences in CPR 36.17 do not apply. It may still have Calderbank effect.

## Costs consequences

**Claimant's Part 36 beaten at trial (CPR 36.17(4)):**
- Interest on the sum awarded at up to 10% above base rate from the end of the relevant period.
- Indemnity costs from the end of the relevant period.
- Interest on those costs at up to 10% above base rate.
- An additional amount: 10% of the first £500,000 awarded, plus 5% of the next £500,000 (capped at £75,000).

**Defendant's Part 36 not beaten by claimant (CPR 36.17(3)):**
- Defendant gets costs from the end of the relevant period on the standard basis (no indemnity escalation).
- Claimant pays interest on those costs.

**Calderbank in ET:** The ET may take an unaccepted offer into account when considering costs, but costs are awarded only where a party has acted vexatiously, abusively, disruptively, unreasonably, or the claim had no reasonable prospect of success (ETR 2013 rule 76). The costs pressure from a Calderbank in the ET is therefore real but limited.

**ACAS COT3:** A binding settlement once signed — not a costs-pressure mechanism. Once signed, the claim is settled and cannot be brought in the ET.

## Inputs to gather

- Forum (ET / civil court).
- Whether ACAS is involved.
- Who is making the offer (claimant or respondent).
- Sum offered. Whether it is inclusive or exclusive of costs.
- What the offer covers (whole claim / specific heads / specific issues).
- Tax treatment — see Tax note below.
- Relevant period (default 21 days for Part 36; common Calderbank period is 21 days).
- Counterclaim, if any (Part 36 must address it).

## Tax note

Settlement of employment claims has specific tax treatment. ITEPA 2003 ss.401-416: termination payments are taxable above £30,000. PILON is fully taxable as earnings under s.402B (introduced by F(No.2)A 2017 — PENP regime). Injury-to-feelings awards in discrimination cases may be tax-free if the discriminatory act preceded termination (Moorthy v HMRC; HMRC guidance is contested — flag for tax advice). ## Workflow

### Step 1 — Confirm forum and vehicle
Infer the forum from the matter context (claim number format, pleaded jurisdiction). Surface for confirmation only if ambiguous: Employment Tribunal or civil court, and whether ACAS is involved. Choose Part 36 / Calderbank / COT3 from the decision tree above.

### Step 2 — Settle the figure and the heads
What sum, for which claims, on what basis (with or without admission, inclusive or exclusive of costs to date).

### Step 3 — Draft

Use the relevant template below. Flag the costs consequences explicitly in the covering letter — the recipient should not need to look them up.

## Output templates

### Template A — CPR Part 36 offer (civil courts)

**WITHOUT PREJUDICE SAVE AS TO COSTS**
**OFFER UNDER CPR PART 36**

[Date]

[Recipient]

Dear Sirs,

**Re: [Case name and claim number]**

This letter contains an offer to settle under Part 36 of the Civil Procedure Rules.

1. This offer is made pursuant to Part 36 CPR.
2. It relates to [the whole of the claim / the claim for [head]].
3. It [takes / does not take] account of [the counterclaim / the cross-claim].
4. The relevant period for the purposes of CPR 36.5(1)(c) is 21 days from the date of this letter.
5. The offer is: [our client offers to pay the claimant £[amount] in full and final settlement of the claim / our client offers to accept £[amount] in full and final settlement of the claim], such sum to be paid within 14 days of acceptance.
6. [State whether the figure is inclusive or exclusive of interest, costs, and any deductions.]

If this offer is accepted within the relevant period, the costs consequences in CPR 36.13 apply. If it is not accepted and our client is later awarded a judgment at least as advantageous as this offer, our client will rely on CPR 36.17 in support of an application for indemnity costs and enhanced interest.

Yours faithfully,

[Signature]

### Template B — Calderbank offer (Employment Tribunal)

**WITHOUT PREJUDICE SAVE AS TO COSTS**

[Date]

Dear Sirs,

**Re: [Case name and ET claim number]**

This is a settlement offer made without prejudice save as to costs.

1. Our client offers [to pay / to accept] £[amount] in full and final settlement of all claims arising from the employment of [name] by [respondent], including but not limited to [list heads].
2. The offer is open for acceptance for 21 days from the date of this letter.
3. If accepted, the sum is to be paid within 14 days. Settlement is to be recorded by [ACAS COT3 / settlement agreement under s.203 ERA 1996]. Note that without an ACAS COT3 or a compliant settlement agreement, an ET claim cannot be validly settled — see s.203 ERA 1996.
4. The offer is inclusive of [interest / costs to date / pension loss / injury to feelings] — specify.

If the offer is not accepted, our client reserves the right to rely on this letter on the question of costs under ETR 2013 rule 76 in due course.

Yours faithfully,

[Signature]

### Template C — ACAS COT3 instructions (not the COT3 itself — that is drafted by the ACAS conciliator)

Provide to the ACAS conciliator: parties, sum, breakdown by head (PILON, ex-gratia, injury to feelings, etc.), tax treatment per head, payment date, claims covered (use broad wording — "all claims arising from the employment and its termination known or unknown"), confidentiality, references, tax indemnity. Confirm the claimant has been advised on the agreement. ACAS will draft the COT3 from these instructions.

## What this skill does not do

- Negotiate. The skill drafts the vehicle; counsel and client run the negotiation.
- Draft the substantive settlement agreement — see `settlement-agreement-review` for the s.203 framework.
- Apply Scotland (where Part 36 does not apply at all and Pursuers' / Defenders' Offers / Tenders apply).
